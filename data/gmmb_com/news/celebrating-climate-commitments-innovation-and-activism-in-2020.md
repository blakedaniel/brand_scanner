**Hot Topic: Hope.** As we approach the end of an unprecedented year of climate extremes, including a historic wildfire season in the West and a record-setting storm season in the Gulf and East coasts, the effects of the climate crisis are more intense and visible than ever. And yet, thanks to a number of developments in 2020 that signal a growing commitment to curbing the worst effects of climate change, the world may be approaching a turning point on climate action. Below are the top six climate moves from the past year that give us hope for 2021 and beyond.

Initiatives in [New York](https://urldefense.proofpoint.com/v2/url?u=https-3A__mailchimp.us4.list-2Dmanage.com_track_click-3Fu-3Df2f8c4bdabe1a2a83f914e813-26id-3Da79b722fbf-26e-3D990aa0b3ac&d=DwMFaQ&c=HdAUNv_EOZyljLc1cjbHCq-Eo7r1kRHoywhQbi81uaA&r=aHravb73KeI42DTXL4n05Y3X1-muuB4FR-OxOtTYSkM&m=KAg2exPV5iYJGNP7nhBNJ3n-vf9g1BaquQZV-QJsmZw&s=9iLCare0ilE324H2QTl5W4VpgpUjX0etAWN7TYDR30o&e=) and [Texas](https://urldefense.proofpoint.com/v2/url?u=https-3A__mailchimp.us4.list-2Dmanage.com_track_click-3Fu-3Df2f8c4bdabe1a2a83f914e813-26id-3Dff5444a54e-26e-3D990aa0b3ac&d=DwMFaQ&c=HdAUNv_EOZyljLc1cjbHCq-Eo7r1kRHoywhQbi81uaA&r=aHravb73KeI42DTXL4n05Y3X1-muuB4FR-OxOtTYSkM&m=KAg2exPV5iYJGNP7nhBNJ3n-vf9g1BaquQZV-QJsmZw&s=eNIS2rMOX1evOTxI_MThEchQwGjbUd-OT_B6O4WV1_k&e=) will ensure climate funding for low-income communities and communities of color. And the first round of funding from the [Bezos Earth Fund](https://urldefense.proofpoint.com/v2/url?u=https-3A__mailchimp.us4.list-2Dmanage.com_track_click-3Fu-3Df2f8c4bdabe1a2a83f914e813-26id-3D7ee0eb102e-26e-3D990aa0b3ac&d=DwMFaQ&c=HdAUNv_EOZyljLc1cjbHCq-Eo7r1kRHoywhQbi81uaA&r=aHravb73KeI42DTXL4n05Y3X1-muuB4FR-OxOtTYSkM&m=KAg2exPV5iYJGNP7nhBNJ3n-vf9g1BaquQZV-QJsmZw&s=ZoxweawEFSvV6JvDOvcq1icbl2r2wV95uaGzjiMQv4Q&e=) included a historic $151 million in grants to organizations focused exclusively on environmental justice, which have historically received less than one percent of philanthropic climate funding.
6. **Climate activism among youth.** Youth activists were among the most vocal supporters of climate action this year, moving from the in-person marches and demonstrations of 2019 to new forms of protest. From the [classroom](https://urldefense.proofpoint.com/v2/url?u=https-3A__mailchimp.us4.list-2Dmanage.com_track_click-3Fu-3Df2f8c4bdabe1a2a83f914e813-26id-3Dabca30d805-26e-3D990aa0b3ac&d=DwMFaQ&c=HdAUNv_EOZyljLc1cjbHCq-Eo7r1kRHoywhQbi81uaA&r=aHravb73KeI42DTXL4n05Y3X1-muuB4FR-OxOtTYSkM&m=KAg2exPV5iYJGNP7nhBNJ3n-vf9g1BaquQZV-QJsmZw&s=z02RlPtdlIaaSZtHmCbpzWv2cs6muhBRQKCyF5H7Y3Y&e=) to their [job searches](https://urldefense.proofpoint.com/v2/url?u=https-3A__mailchimp.us4.list-2Dmanage.com_track_click-3Fu-3Df2f8c4bdabe1a2a83f914e813-26id-3D2d0a8a0e6c-26e-3D990aa0b3ac&d=DwMFaQ&c=HdAUNv_EOZyljLc1cjbHCq-Eo7r1kRHoywhQbi81uaA&r=aHravb73KeI42DTXL4n05Y3X1-muuB4FR-OxOtTYSkM&m=KAg2exPV5iYJGNP7nhBNJ3n-vf9g1BaquQZV-QJsmZw&s=lM7WmllYGjhUzVKLYBspOpT30WyGS0uBxYdscAf0dEQ&e=), young people advocated for environmental justice and took a stance against companies who support fossil fuel behemoths. And the adults are listening—young activists on the UN [Youth Advisory Group on Climate Change](https://urldefense.proofpoint.com/v2/url?u=https-3A__mailchimp.us4.list-2Dmanage.com_track_click-3Fu-3Df2f8c4bdabe1a2a83f914e813-26id-3Dd34adf30b7-26e-3D990aa0b3ac&d=DwMFaQ&c=HdAUNv_EOZyljLc1cjbHCq-Eo7r1kRHoywhQbi81uaA&r=aHravb73KeI42DTXL4n05Y3X1-muuB4FR-OxOtTYSkM&m=KAg2exPV5iYJGNP7nhBNJ3n-vf9g1BaquQZV-QJsmZw&s=BFM0NsdZ5aya7yMPI7-nZ0NY2iVnjfnDALun9cxq0_8&e=) now advise the secretary-general on climate action.
